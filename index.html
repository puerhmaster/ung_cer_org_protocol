    Introduction¶

    Cerebral organoids are three-dimensional multicellular structures derived from pluripotent stem cells that mimic brain-like regions. This protocol outlines the generation of “unguided” cerebral organoids from induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs), allowing spontaneous formation of dorsal forebrain–like areas. Applications include studying embryonic brain development, species-specific neurogenesis differences, and modeling neurodevelopmental disorders.

    Materials and Equipment¶

    Cell Lines and Media¶

    • iPSC Lines: Human, chimpanzee, or rhesus monkey
    • iPSC Culture Media: mTeSR Plus (human/chimpanzee) or iPSC Brew (macaque)
    • Neuroinduction Media (NIM): DMEM/F12 + supplements
    • Differentiation Media (DM): DMEM/F12 + Neurobasal + supplements, ± vitamin A

    Reagents¶

    • DPBS (Dulbecco’s Phosphate-Buffered Saline)
    • Accutase
    • DMEM/F-12
    • Pro-survival compound (PSC) or CET + Polyamine (CET+P)
    • Basement membrane matrix (Matrigel)
    • Retinoic acid (vitamin A)

    Anti-apoptotic Inhibitors Compositions¶

    Component Dilution
    PSC 1:1000
    CET 1:4000
    Polyamine 1:1000

    Plastics and Disposables¶

    • 96-well ultralow attachment plates
    • 15 mL conical tubes, 60 mm Petri dishes
    • Parafilm, sterile scissors, forceps, wide-bore pipette tips
    • Neubauer chamber or automated cell counter

    Equipment¶

    • Laminar Flow Hood: UV sterilization & sterile work
    • Incubator: 37 °C, 5 % CO₂, 95 % humidity
    • Centrifuge: 300 × g capability
    • Orbital Shaker: ~65 rpm

    Pre-Protocol Considerations¶

    1. iPSC Quality: Minimal spontaneous differentiation; mycoplasma-free
    2. Passaging: Aim for ~80 % confluency on EB-seeding day
    3. Sterile Technique: Disinfect with 70–80 % ethanol

    Detailed Protocol¶

    Timeline Overview¶


    Figure: Schematic overview of organoid production timeline.

    Stage I: Cell Cultivation and Seeding (Day 0)¶

    Stem Cell Cultivation¶

    Maintain iPSCs until ~80 % confluency; split 1:6–1:15. Limit to three passages to preserve genomic stability.


    Figure: Cultivation of induced pluripotent stem cells.

    Dissociation of iPSCs¶

    1. Aspirate medium at 80–90 % confluence.
    2. Wash with DPBS.
    3. Add 400 µL Accutase; incubate 37 °C for 5–7 min.
    4. Confirm detachment; add 1 mL prewarmed DMEM/F12 (± anti-apoptotic).
    5. Pipette 5–10× to single-cell suspension.

    Harvesting and Counting Cells¶

    See Appendix A for counting details.

    1. Transfer suspension to 15 mL tube.
    2. Centrifuge 300 × g, 5 min, RT.
    3. Resuspend pellet in 2 mL DMEM/F12 (± PSC).
    4. Count via hemocytometer or automated counter.

    Seeding into 96-Well Plate¶

    See Appendix A for calculations.

    1. Adjust to 9,000 cells/150 µL (60,000 cells/mL).
    2. Dispense 150 µL/well in ultralow-attachment 96-well plate.
    3. Gently mix suspension before each pipetting.
    4. Incubate 37 °C, 5 % CO₂, undisturbed for 48 h.
    5. At 2 dps, remove 100 µL and add 150 µL fresh medium (without PSC).

    Media Change Technique (2 dps)¶

    • Remove Medium: Tilt plate; angle P200 tip ~45 ° above EB; aspirate 100 µL.
    • Add Medium: Dispense 150 µL down well wall to avoid turbulence.


    Figure: Seeding of 9,000 cells per well.

    Stage II: Embryoid Body Growth¶

    1. Change 150 µL medium every other day (angle tip to avoid EBs).
    2. By 4–5 dps, EBs appear smooth & spherical (~150–300 µm).
    3. Inspect for contamination; discard compromised wells.

    Stage III: Neuroinduction¶

    1. Switch to NIM on day 4 (macaque) or day 5 (human/chimpanzee).
    2. Replace 150 µL every other day; prewarm media & pipette carefully.

    Stage IV: Embedding in Basement Membrane Matrix (Days 7–9)¶

    1. Thaw Matrigel on ice (~1.5 mL for 96 EBs).
    2. Cut Parafilm to ~6 × 6 cm; press over tip rack to form 4×4 dimples; trim.
    3. Place Parafilm in 60 mm dish, dimples up.

    Transferring & Embedding¶

    1. Tilt EB plate; collect EBs to one side.
    2. Use cut 200 µL tip (2–3 mm cut) for gentle aspiration (150–200 µL).
    3. Deposit one EB per dimple; aspirate excess medium with 20 µL tip.
    4. Add cold Matrigel droplet over each EB; center with 10 µL tip.


    Figure: Transferring EBs to Parafilm for embedding.


    Figure: Embedding EBs in Matrigel for 3D support.

    1. Polymerize 37 °C, 15–30 min.
    2. Invert Parafilm into DM (no vitamin A); swirl to release EBs.
    3. Transfer to shaker: 65 rpm, 37 °C, 5 % CO₂, 95 % humidity.

    Post-Embedding Maintenance (Full-Volume Changes)¶

    • Change full volume (5–6 mL) every other day.
    • Gently swirl dish to center organoids before aspiration.
    • Refill down dish wall; avoid shear stress.
    • Monitor for fusion; separate gently with wide-bore tip.


    Figure: Organoid growth on shaker (no vitamin A).

    Stage V: Retinoic Acid Addition & Ventricle Formation (Days 13–15+)¶

    1. Switch to DM + vitamin A on day 13 (rhesus) or 14–15 (human/chimpanzee).
    2. Full‐volume changes every 3–4 days (2–3 days if needed).
    3. Expect ventricle‐like cavities in 1–2 weeks; monitor fusion.


    Figure: Organoid growth post‐retinoic acid.

    Final Remarks: Follow sterile technique, monitor morphology, and adjust timeline per cell line/species for optimal results.

    Appendix A: Counting Cells in a Neubauer Chamber¶

    Accurate counting ensures reproducible organoid formation. Mix 10 µL cell suspension + 10 µL trypan blue (1:1; 2× dilution). Load 10 µL under coverslip.

    Microscopy & Counting¶

    • Count four corner squares; include top/left boundaries only.
    • Viability (%) = (unstained / total) × 100.

    Concentration Calculations¶

    1. Average cells/square (e.g. (45+50+48+52)/4 = 48.75).
    2. Cells/mL = 48.75×10⁴ = 4.875×10⁵.
    3. Correct for dye (2×) → 9.75×10⁵ cells/mL.

    Preparing for 96-Well Plate¶

    • Target 9,000 cells/150 µL → 60,000 cells/mL.
    • Dilution factor = (9.75×10⁵)/(6.0×10⁴) ≈ 16.25.
    • Mix 1 mL stock + 15.25 mL medium → 16.25 mL.
    • Dispense 150 µL/well for 96 wells (14.4 mL), with margin.

    Appendix B: Preventing & Managing Sticking of Organoids¶

    A. Preventive Measures¶

    • ≤16 organoids per 60 mm dish.
    • Even Parafilm dimples & proper Matrigel set.
    • Gentle shaking (65 rpm).
    • Wide-bore tips for transfers.

    B. Managing Stuck/Fused Organoids¶

    • Gently swirl or pipette medium onto fusion.
    • Use minimal force; coax apart.

    C. Additional Tips¶

    • Inspect daily; intervene early.
    • Keep log of events & fixes.
